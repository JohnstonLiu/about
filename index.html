Why do Students Procrastinate? How do You Stop It?

Time management is a problem that most people will have faced at least once in their lifetime. In most cases, time management issues begin to rise during one's high school years. Where students would previously lead a carefree life, once academics are starting to be taken more seriously and workloads increase, time management will be essential to their success. However, this is where most students will suffer.


The Root Cause

Procrastination can be defined as "the action of delaying or postponing something". Now using this definition we can realize that in theory we would only delay things if they were unenjoyable. Now we can either make this work enjoyable or we can help ourselves realize the importance of this work. In order to make the work enjoyable you can treat it like a game. Whenever you finish a task, give yourself a reward. However, make sure that your reward is NOT distraction. It can be a snack, working on a personal project, or a hobby just for a few minutes before you get back to work. And before you know it, once you have completed all your task, you will feel this immense ammount of satisfaction. For once, your to do list is finally empty. However, if it is simply impossible for you to make the work enjoyable, then we must treat them as Gravity Problems.


Gravity Problems

In this Ted Talk Bill Burnett introduces the concept of Gravity Problems and how you can deal with them. To put it simply, Gravity Problems are problems that we cannot combat. They are a circumstance that we are in and a circumstance that we must accept. For example, say you really hate learning a certain course. The topic is bland and you feel as if you are just wasting time on it. Well you know what, you will just have to deal with it. You will have to acknowledge it as a Gravity Problem and accept that it will just be a way of life. Just like brushing your teeth every morning or dealing with an annoying teacher there is nothing you can do about it and there is nothing you should do. Nevertheless, there are problems that are not Gravity Problems and they can be combatted.


Social Media

With most cases of procrastination, the main perpetrator can be linked to social media. Either your friend is messaging you or there is this new video on YouTube that you must watch, these urges are what you need to break. A simple way is to turn off notiffications. I cannot stress how simple yet effective this is. However, that may not be enough. Say you completed an hour of your studies and you decided to take a break. You instinctively grab your phone and hop onto social media. Where you previously stated that it would only be five minutes, the enjoyment that social media provides is too much to give up. The five minutes turn into ten minutes, then to thirty minutes, until you reach an hour. You suddenly check the time only to see you have wasted an hour and not realized it. Now it is time to start breaking your habits.


Breaking Habits

In order to stop social media from controlling your life. You first need to take control. There are many ways to approach this and I will list a few effective ones. Everytime you pick up your phone, ask yourself "How will this benefit my future?" If that does not work, you can be physical about it and place your phone away out of convenience to where you work. This way whenever you instinctively reach for your phone, you realize that it is not there and that you placed it far away in order to combat your procrastination. Which reminds you that you should be getting to work. However, if your distraction is not just your phone but rather all forms of technology, there is still a way. Any work that can be completed without technology should be done away from technology. Move away from your desk and move to the kitchen, or you could even go outside. However if technology is required for your work, you can apply the same principle by asking yourself "How will this benefit my future?" whenever you click on a distraction. If self-motivation is not enough, there is once again a brute force option. A free desktop app called "Cold Turkey" can block distracting websites with no way for you to disable it. Uninstalling, ending with Task Manager, Force Quiting, etc has no effect. However, if you are unable to implement the methods mentioned previously, there is still hope.

Exceptions

If you keep forgetting to ask yourself "How will this benefit my future?" everytime you get distracted, have a poster in your workspace or a wallpaper on your computer or phone or something to remind you. If you are unable to place your phone at an inconvenience to you, shut down your phone once you get to work. That way whenever you try to use your phone, you have to think before turning it on to which you will remember to ask yourself, "How will this benefit my future?". Another method is to move around all social media icons on your phone frequently. This way whenever you are searching for your distraction, you will once again remember, "How will this benefit my future?".


Closing Remarks

Students procrastinate because they do not enjoy their work. To combat this we can either make the work enjoyable or treat it as a Gravity Problem. We must learn to accept Gravity Problems as a way of life and to simply deal with it. Social media is another problem that we face but it is not a Gravity Problem. We can combat social media by breaking our habits through self-motivation and if needed brute force. Hopefully this discussion can help you understand why students procrastinate and how procrastination can be combatted.
